What is

PhyloDiver?

PhyloDiver is a interactive tool for visualizing and exploring evolutionary phylogenies constructed from analyses of genetic variations in tumor samples. This tool was designed to simultaneously enable end-users to examine mutation histories while maintaining sample proportions of different subclone populations.

PhyloDiver's unique visualizations also connect to the underlying annotated mutations allowing for a comprehensive examination of the history, composition, and proportion of clonal subpopulations in cancer tumors.

How It Works

Analysis Overview

Many cancer phylogenics workflows analyze somatic variants from bulk sequencing of normal and tumor tissues. These workflows identify clusters of related mutations and then construct an evolutionary tree (phylogeny) that explains the occurrence patterns of those mutation clusters in the sample data. PhyloDiver visualizes the predicted tumor mutational evolution, summarizes the unique mutations along each edge, and shows the cellular proportions of each cancer subclone.

Data Exploration

PhyloDiver includes many features that enable users to explore the tree results and the genes and mutations present in the data. Users can click on any edge between nodes to scan mutation, gene and drug information and expand any edge to a full table display.
